WATER TESTING

Water quality is defined by analyzing it in terms of its: -

1. Chemical content: - Hardness (Ca + Mg), metals (Fe, etc), nutrients (N and P), Cl, Na, organic compounds, etc..
2. Physical content: - Turbidity, color, odor, etc.
3. Biological content: - Fecal coliform, total coliform, viruses.

Water quality standards of a few - mg/L: -

Total organic carbon 0.05 Purified H2O
Ar 0.05 MCLG
Cu 1.3 MCLG
Pb Zero MCLG
Hg 0.002 MCLG
Cl 250 Purified H2O
Fe 0.3 Purified H2O
Mg 0.05 Purified H2O

MCLG = Maximum Contaminant Level Goal

Laboratory work: -

(i) Determination of pH
(ii) BOD
(iii) COD
(iv) Dissolved pollutants and salts

Water quality parameters and indicators: -

1. Temperatures: - Temperature is important as it affects the amount of oxygen dissolved in it.
2. Salinity
3. pH

it is measured by using a calorimetric test.
Litmus paper, phenolphthalein, methyl orange, or universal indicator can be used.
Acceptable pH value is 6.5 to 8.5 on pH scale.

4. BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand) and COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand): -

Under it, oxygen amount dissolved in H2O is measured. It is variable in temperature.

5. Specific Conductance
