DEFINITION

Natural resource economics deals with the supply, demand, and allocation of the Earth's natural resources. One main objective of natural resource economics is to better understand the role of natural resources in the economy in order to develop more sustainable methods of managing those resources to ensure their availability to future generations.

(http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_resource_economics)

Resources: -

Natural resources are the features of natural environment that are of value in serving human needs, or any other organism's.

(https://www.google.co.in/search?hl=en&noj=1&q=natural+resources&tbs=dfn:1)
(http://scherervillegardenclub.org/glossary.html)

Scarcity and Growth: -

Various factors such as pollution, overuse, misuse of natural resources result in their scarcity. If there is no pollution and the natural resources are used sustainably, they will certainly grow.

A country's economy, beauty, health & biodiversity's growth is directly proportional to the growth of its natural resources.

Natural resource accounting: -

Natural resource accounting is an accounting system that deals with stocks and stock changes of natural assets, comprising biota [combined flora and fauna of a region] (produced or wild), subsoil assets [a useful or valuable quality of anything] (proved reserves), water and land with their aquatic and terrestrial [of or relating to the earth & its inhabitants] ecosystems. 

It is frequently used in the sense of physical accounting as distinguished from monetary (environmental) accounting.

(http://stats.oecd.org/glossary/detail.asp?ID=1739)

Classification of Natural Resources: -

Non-renewable resources Renewable resources

Nonrenewable resources are those natural resources that cannot be replaced once they are used up. Some examples include:
• oil
• coal
• gas
• uranium

Also called:
• conventional resources
• exhaustible resources

Renewable resources are those resources that can be replaced as they are used up. Some examples include:
• soil
• air
• water
• wood
• sunlight

Also called:
• non-conventional resources
• inexhaustible resources

Physical accounting: -

Physical accounting refers to the natural resource and environmental accounting of stocks and changes in stocks in physical (non-monetary) units, for example, weight, area or number. Qualitative measures, expressed in terms of quality classes, types of uses or ecosystem characteristics, may supplement quantitative measures. The combined changes in asset quality and quantity are called volume changes.

(http://stats.oecd.org/glossary/detail.asp?ID=2061)

Advantages and Disadvantages of Natural resource Accounting / Reporting: -

Advantages:

• Reporting is reliable and large fluctuations are minimized.
• Reporting is clear and not misleading.
• Management's discretion on the reported amounts is minimized.
• Reported amounts are accurate and verifiable.

Disadvantages:

• There is no recognition of assets available for sale.
• No reporting is provided on the face of the financial statements, although full reporting is obtained through the footnotes and stewardship reporting.
