FEMINISM

Feminism is a collection of movements and ideologies aimed at defining, establishing, and defending equal political, economic, and social rights for women. In addition, feminism seeks to establish equal opportunities for women and employment.

(http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feminism)

Feminist: - A feminist is an advocate or supporter of the rights and equality of women.

Feminism in India: -

Feminists in India have struggled to seek
     1. Gender equality
     2. Right to work at equal wages
     3. Right to equal access to health and education
     4. Equal political rights
     5. Inheritance laws
     6. Abolition of Sati, widow immolation

The history of feminism in India can be divided into 3 phases: -
     1. When European colonists began to seek out for sati.
     2. When M.K. Gandhi incorporated women's movements to Quit India Movement and independent women's organizations began to emerge.
     3. At post-Independence which has focused on fair treatment of women in the work force and right to political party.

Despite of progress made by Indian Feminists, women living in present India are facing many issues of discrimination. In past two decades, there has also emerged a disturbing trend of sex selective abortion (female foeticide) and female infanticide.

(http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feminism_in_India)
